This is a lemma that is used to bound the number of sections in a line bundle over a projective surface. 

\begin{lemma}
Let $Y$ be a smooth projective irreducible surface over a field $k$. Let $B$ be big and nef line bundle on $Y$. Then for very line bundle $L$ on $Y$, 
$$ h^0(Y, L) \le \frac{(L \cdot B)^2}{2 B \cdot B} + O(L \cdot B) + O(1) $$
where the implied constants depend on $Y$ and $B$, but not $L$. 
\end{lemma}


\begin{proposition}
Let $X$ be a 2-dimensional closed integral subscheme of $\IP^n$ over $\IF_q$. For $f$ in a set of density $1$, there is a bijection $\Irr X_\IF \to \Irr (X_f)_\IF$ sending $C$ to $C \cap X_f$. 
\end{proposition}
The bijection says to two things: a generic hypersurface will intersect each componenet, but the intersection with each component will be irreducible. In fact, Lemma~\ref{vanish} implies that since $X$ has only finitely many irreducible components, this statement is no stronger than saying that if $X$ is an irreducible, then for $f$ in a subset of density 1, $H_f \cap X$ is irreducible: We know that two irreducible surfaces will intersect at curves, but we may neglect those hypersurfaces that vanish idenitically on one of those curves.
